Project Guidelines
Team Project
Throughout this course you will complete a team project where you will work on a problem from a Deep Learning perspective with at least one of your classmates.  You will be randomly assigned to teams.  (If you have not been assigned to a team by the 2nd week of class, please contact your instructor).

The project will consist of three parts (Specific details are in the section below):

Project Proposal submitted during Week 5 of the course.
Project Deliverables:  Report, PowerPoint Slides, Code, etc (see details below)
Video Presentation (video recording)
Each student will also submit an Peer Evaluation Form for Group Project  to evaluate your collaboration with your team.

 

Project Objectives
Defining and developing a deep learning project is an important objective in this course. Teams are encouraged to define their own data science problems and demonstrate how to solve it with deep neural networks. This includes the collection and processing of datasets, building neural networks, train and evaluate their performance.

The first step is to collect or find datasets relevant to your project. You have many options. One option is to work on real-world data related to your job to get new insights about a problem of interest to your employer. Another option is to find dataset online. Fortunately, many open-data source platforms provide large datasets, covering a wide range of applications. You might be interested to check websites such as:

KaggleLinks to an external site.
Google Dataset SearchLinks to an external site.
Awesome Public DatasetsLinks to an external site.
etc.
You are allowed to download and re-use existing datasets and and extended code of existing projects. In all cases, you should explicitly refer to the source of your datasets and explain how did/will you re-use code of existing projects. All projects submissions will be verified by TurnitinLinks to an external site. to check the originality of your work.

Defining the project is an iterative process, So, don't hesitate to contact me to discuss your ideas and frame your project objectives.

 

Project Proposal
Note: if you choose any topic on time-serial data, such as stock price prediction, please also add an extra section on your proposal "Data Leaking (time-serial data project only)".  In the section, you have to discuss (1) what is data leaking and (2) what is the common data leaking mistakes often made by beginners who work on time serial data.

Your team will decided on a project and submit a proposal during the first part of the course.  (See due-date in Syllabus).  

The project proposal will include:

Project Title
Team members
The Problem from the Deep Learning Perspective
Description
Challenges
is there any available solution to this problem?
Dataset: source, size, features description,  etc
Deep Learning process
Choices of Deep Neural Networks and their justification
Training/Testing strategy
Evaluation metrics
Expected results
References to  data source
Justification of reusing existing code
The project proposal is due by the end of week 5 to give more time to become familiar with various deep neural networks and problems.

Please download the project proposal template, which is a general-purpose outline for a typical deep learning project proposal. Feel free to update and adapted to your project structure. Each section has a list of items to help you develop and expand upon the corresponding content.

 

Deliverables
After receiving feedback on your proposal from your instructor, you can begin working on the project.  The final project will be due at the end of the course and will consist of an oral presentation with PowerPoint slides, as well as a written report.

Report: The project write-up should be based on the Project Template.  It should be between 10+ pages and include:
Project Title
Problem Description
Dataset Description and its source
A short Description of  functionalities and services performed by your code.
An Individual Feedback Page on learned lessons, difficulties and collaboration with teammates.
Report size should be at least 8 pages, including input from the technical survey.
PowerPoint Slides: You can use the PowerPoint Template  as a guide to prepare your PowerPoint slides for the presentation.
Source Code: Python Scripts or Jupyter Notebook
The project submission is one single zipped file to Canvas including:

 Source code: Python scripts or Jupyter Notebook
Oral Presentation Slides (.ppt format)
Write up report, including, project title, list of team members, the project objectives, and major outcomes (.doc format)
 

Video Presentation
Prepare your presentation covering all the points in write-up.  You may record your presentation using ZoomLinks to an external site. or KalturaLinks to an external site..  If you need any assistance using these technologies, please contact the Penn State IT Help DeskLinks to an external site..

Please note: 

This is a team presentation, therefore both teammates should present and include a demonstration.
Approximate length of video: 25 minutes
Submit your presentation to the Project Presentation (Video) Discussion by the date indicated in the Course Schedule.  
Each team will be required to view and provide feedback on at least one other team's presentation.  (Team 1 will evaluate Team 2, and Team 2 will evaluate Team 3, and Team 3 will evaluate Team 4... and so on.  The last Team will evaluate Team 1).  Each member of the team will provide individual feedback to include: 3 points that summarize the strengths of the project and 3 points to indicate the potential issues to improve. The evaluation might also include questions about the project and its results. 
You may also view the other teams' presentations as well. 
Be sure to view and reply to the peer evaluation posts made on your video presentation by your classmates.

 

PS: The Video Presentation will also be uploaded with Deliverables to a Shared Folder provided by the instructor.
